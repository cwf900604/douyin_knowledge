# 6 Professional B2B Outreach Templates — English Edition

**Company:** Yuanyu Drive (Shenzhen) Technology Co., Ltd.
Chinese manufacturer & exporter specializing in servo motors — RC hobby,
industrial, brushless, high-torque, waterproof, RS485 bus servos.
Brands: **GX Precision** (competition-grade) & **YP Motion** (professional value).

**Target Audience:** B2B wholesale buyers, distributors, dealers, OEM procurement managers.

**Tone:** Professional, relationship-first, native English business tone. Not spammy.
No ALL CAPS hype. No excessive exclamation marks.

---

## Template 1: LinkedIn Connection Request (300 char limit)

**When to use:** After viewing a prospect's profile. You share a group, industry,
or mutual connection. Goal: get accepted, NOT to sell immediately.

### Variant A — Shared Industry

> Hi [FIRST_NAME], I came across your profile through [MUTUAL_GROUP/MUTUAL_CONNECTION].
> I'm also in the [SERVO_MOTOR / ENERGY_STORAGE] space and admire what [THEIR_COMPANY]
> is doing. Would be great to connect and follow your work.

### Variant B — Event/Trade Show Lead

> Hi [FIRST_NAME], saw we both attended [EVENT_NAME / FAIR_NAME]. I work with
> Yuanyu Drive (Shenzhen) Technology Co., Ltd. in [PRODUCT_CATEGORY] manufacturing. Would love to stay in touch —
> always keen to exchange industry perspectives with peers.

### Variant C — Content/Post Engagement

> Hi [FIRST_NAME], your recent post on [TOPIC] resonated — especially the point
> about [SPECIFIC_POINT]. I'm on the manufacturing side of [PRODUCT_CATEGORY]
> here in China. Would be great to connect and keep up with your insights.

**Tip:** Always personalize `[THEIR_COMPANY]` and `[TOPIC]`. Never send generic.
If you don't have a mutual connection, reference a recent post or company milestone.

## Template 3: Cold Email — Subject Lines + Body

**When to use:** Targeted cold outreach to procurement managers, sourcing directors,
or distributor owners. Research the recipient first. Reference their company
specifically in the email.

### Subject Line Options (A/B Test These)

| # | Subject Line | Best For |
|---|-------------|----------|
| A | Sourcing [PRODUCT_TYPE]? — Yuanyu Drive (Shenzhen) Technology Co., Ltd. catalog | Direct, category-aware buyers |
| B | [FIRST_NAME], quick question re: your [PRODUCT] supply chain | Curiosity-driven open |
| C | Met at [EVENT]? / Referred by [NAME] — servo & battery OEM supply | Warm-lead only |

### Email Body (Use with any subject line)

> Hi [FIRST_NAME],
>
> I'm reaching out because I saw that [THEIR_COMPANY] works with [PRODUCT_TYPE /
> APPLICATION], and I wanted to introduce ourselves briefly.
>
> I'm [YOUR_TITLE] at Yuanyu Drive (Shenzhen) Technology Co., Ltd., a manufacturer and exporter based in
> [CITY], China. We specialize in two product lines:
>
> **Servo Motors** — RC hobby, industrial brushless, high-torque models for
> automation, robotics, drone propulsion, and CNC applications.
>
> **New Energy Batteries** — LiFePO4 cells, energy storage systems, and solar
> batteries for residential, commercial, and off-grid use.
>
> We supply wholesale buyers, distributors, and OEMs in [REGIONS]. Our standard
> terms include:
> - MOQ as low as [NUMBER] units (negotiable for trial orders)
> - Samples available — you cover freight, we cover the unit
> - CIF / FOB / EXW — flexible depending on your preference
> - Payment: T/T (30/70), L/C at sight
>
> If [PRODUCT_TYPE] is something you're currently sourcing — or might source in
> the next quarter — I'd be glad to send over our latest catalog and a competitive
> quote for your reference.
>
> If the timing isn't right, no problem at all. I'll check back in a couple of
> months unless you'd rather I didn't.
>
> Thanks for your time, [FIRST_NAME].
>
> Best regards,
> [YOUR_FULL_NAME]
> [TITLE]
> Yuanyu Drive (Shenzhen) Technology Co., Ltd.
> [WEBSITE / ALIBABA STORE]
> [WHATSAPP] | [EMAIL]
> [CITY], China

### Follow-Up Sequence

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 1 | Initial email |
| Day 3-4 | Quick follow-up: "Hi [FIRST_NAME], just checking if the above landed. Happy to send the catalog if useful. [YOUR_NAME]" |
| Day 7-10 | Value-add touch: Share a product sheet PDF or a short market note |
| Day 21 | Last attempt: "Hi [FIRST_NAME], I'll assume the timing isn't right for now. I'll check back next quarter unless you prefer otherwise. [YOUR_NAME]" |

**Rule:** Never send more than 4 touches. If no response, move on. Revisit the
lead after 3-6 months.

## Template 4: Facebook Messenger Opening

**When to use:** For reaching out to cross-border ecommerce sellers, dropshippers,
or small-to-medium importers found in Facebook groups (e.g., RC hobby groups,
solar energy groups, cross-border seller communities).

### Variant A — Direct & Friendly

> Hi [FIRST_NAME]! I came across your post in [GROUP_NAME] and thought it was
> worth saying hello. I work with Yuanyu Drive (Shenzhen) Technology Co., Ltd. — we manufacture [PRODUCT_TYPE]
> here in China and supply sellers and small importers worldwide.
>
> If you ever source [PRODUCT_CATEGORY] from China, I'd be happy to share our
> catalog. No minimums on the first order — we support small-batch trial runs.
>
> Let me know if that's something you'd find useful. Thanks!

### Variant B — Question-Led Opener

> Hey [FIRST_NAME] — saw you're into [HOBBY / NICHE], and I figured you might
> deal with sourcing from time to time. Quick question: do you currently source
> [PRODUCT_TYPE, e.g., brushless motors or LiFePO4 batteries] directly from China?
>
> The reason I ask — we manufacture both at Yuanyu Drive (Shenzhen) Technology Co., Ltd. and work with a lot
> of independent sellers and small importers. Happy to chat if you're ever
> looking for a reliable factory source.
>
> No worries either way. Cheers!

### Variant C — Ultra-Short Cold Open

> Hi [FIRST_NAME] — I'm [YOUR_NAME] with Yuanyu Drive (Shenzhen) Technology Co., Ltd. in China. We make
> [PRODUCT_TYPE] and ship worldwide, small batches welcome. If you ever need a
> reliable factory source for [CATEGORY], let me know. I'll send the catalog
> right here.

**Key principle:** Facebook is casual. Keep it conversational. One question
max. Never send a wall of text. If they reply, move to WhatsApp or email.

## Template 5: TikTok DM / Comment Response

**When to use:** For engaging potential small-batch buyers, hobbyists upgrading
to resellers, or SMB owners who comment on your product videos. Goal: move the
conversation off-platform fast (TikTok DMs are clunky for business).

### Variant A — Response to a Comment (public reply)

> Glad you liked it! We're the factory behind these — DM me and I'll send you
> the details. Or reach me on WhatsApp: [NUMBER]

### Variant B — DM Opener (after they like/follow)

> Hey, thanks for the follow! I saw you're into [NICHE]. We manufacture
> [PRODUCT_TYPE] right here in China — if you ever need a source, I'd be happy
> to send you our catalog. WhatsApp me: [NUMBER] or I can share it here. 🙌

### Variant C — DM to a Creator / Potential Reseller

> Hi! Love your content about [TOPIC]. Quick thought — we manufacture
> [PRODUCT_TYPE] and work with small-batch buyers and content creators who want
> to test or resell. If that sounds interesting, I'm at [WHATSAPP] or
> [EMAIL]. Would love to send a sample your way.

**Rules for TikTok:**
- Keep DMs under 3 sentences. This is NOT the place for a full pitch.
- Always offer a clear off-platform path (WhatsApp is preferred for international).
- If they don't reply, send ONE follow-up after 3-5 days, then stop.
- No links in the first DM (TikTok often flags them).
- Use WhatsApp number format: +86 1XX XXXX XXXX (Chinese number is expected from
  a Chinese factory — it builds credibility).

**Platform-specific notes:**
- **Alibaba:** Attach the datasheet inside the platform chat. Include Trade
  Assurance availability if applicable. Never share external links or contact
  info in the first reply — Alibaba flags this.
- **Global Sources / Made-in-China:** External contact sharing is generally
  tolerated after the first exchange, but keep initial replies on-platform.
- **Reply within 2-4 hours** during business hours (China time). Fast replies
  convert at 3x the rate of delayed ones on B2B platforms.
- **Always attach a product photo + datasheet PDF** in the first reply.
  Buyers often inquire with 5-10 suppliers at once; documents set you apart.

## General Best Practices (All Channels)

| Principle | Practice |
|-----------|----------|
| **Personalize** | Always mention the prospect's company, role, or industry. Never copy-paste blindly. |
| **Value first** | Offer a catalog, sample, market insight, or pricing trend before asking for a call or order. |
| **Right channel** | LinkedIn = relationship. Email = formal pitch. Messenger = casual chat. Platform = fast & complete. |
| **Follow-up cadence** | Max 3-4 touches over 3 weeks. Space them: Day 1, Day 4, Day 10, Day 21. |
| **No spam signals** | Avoid ALL CAPS, excessive `!`, `ACT NOW`, fake urgency. This kills trust with B2B buyers. |
| **Reply speed** | B2B platform inquiries: within 4 hours. Email: within 12 hours. Social DMs: within 24 hours. |
| **File readiness** | Always have your catalog PDF, company profile PDF, and 3-5 hero product photos ready to send. |
| **Phone number format** | Use +86 1XX XXXX XXXX. A Chinese number on WhatsApp is expected and builds factory credibility. |
| **Grammar & tone** | Native-level English. If you're unsure, run your message through an AI polish before sending. |
| **CRM tracking** | Record every outreach in a spreadsheet or CRM: name, company, channel, date, reply status. Follow up methodically. |
